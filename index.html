2023's "5 Best" Vitamin C Serums On The Market

We sorted through hundreds of vitamin C serum products to narrow down the top ones to test. These are the best vitamin C serums to try now.

Vitamin C serums are seemingly everywhere and for good reason—this powerhouse antioxidant and skin brightener is one of the most effective skincare ingredients out there. But vitamin C is notoriously fickle. It can degrade and lose its potency over time or when exposed to light and heat.

Our team of researchers and testers researched and tried hundreds of vitamin C serums on the market to find the best of the best. Testers looked at various factors, including company ethics, quality, efficacy, results and value. They also looked for the most effective forms of vitamin C and other anti-aging ingredients. These “Top 5” best vitamin C serums stood out.

#2 SkinCeuticals

SkinCeuticals C E Ferulic
Overall Rating (4.5)
 4.5/5
  • Quality Rating: 9.1/10
  • Expert Rating: 9.0/10

C E Ferulic serum is one of SkinCeuticals’ most popular products, with a loyal following of aestheticians and dermatologists. Its patented formula provides environmental defense, brightens the skin and helps to improve the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles.

C E Ferulic serum is a clinically tested, award-winning formula with many positive reviews online. Along with vitamin C, vitamin E and ferulic acid, it also contains hyaluronic acid to hydrate the skin.

However, this SkinCeuticals serum contains only 15% L-ascorbic acid (vitamin C). This is less than our top pick. It also skips retinol and botanicals. And at the steep price of $166 per ounce, this cost-prohibitive option is not accessible to all users.

Pros

  • Created by one of the leaders in vitamin C serums
  • Targets fine lines and wrinkles and helps to protect the skin against environmental damage
  • Clinically tested formula with positive reviews and recommendations online
  • Contains L-ascorbic acid, a potent form of vitamin C
  • The company website features clinical before and after photos
  • Easy to purchase at popular online retailers, such as Dermstore.com and Skinstore.com, and in-store at shops like Blue Mercury

Cons

  • Contains a lower concentration of vitamin C than some other top vitamin C serums
  • The formula is missing natural ingredients and some proven anti-agers, like retinol
  • The company is not cruelty free
  • Extremely expensive at $166 per ounce

#3 SkinMedica

SkinMedica Vitamin C+E Complex
Overall Rating (4.3)
 4.3/5
  • Quality Rating: 8.7/10
  • Expert Rating: 8.6/10
  • Users Rating: 8.5/10

SkinMedica Vitamin C + E Complex is one of the simpler formulas we researched, with a focus on vitamins C and E. Our testers liked that it contains two forms of vitamin C: ascorbic acid and tetrahexyldecyl ascorbate. The company states that it delivers this vitamin C to the skin gradually to offer all-day protection.

However, testers also noticed that this serum does not contain ferulic acid. Ferulic acid is widely believed to support the stability and effectiveness of vitamin C formulations. Without it, we have concerns about this formula’s potency and stability.

Also, this serum is not available for sale online, so buying it requires a trip to a dermatologist, aesthetician or plastic surgeon.

Pros

  • Contains two forms of vitamin C: ascorbic acid and tetrahexyldecyl ascorbate
  • Helps to boost the skin’s brightness and improve the appearance of texture and tone
  • Releases antioxidants into the skin gradually for long-lasting daily defense
  • From a professional skincare brand recommended by dermatologists

Cons

  • Does not contain ferulic acid to help preserve potency
  • Does not include other proven anti-aging ingredients like retinol
  • May be difficult to find: SkinMedica products require physician authorization to purchase, so they cannot be purchased online
  • Return policies may vary based on physician office policies

#4 Clarins

Clarins Double Serum
Overall Rating (4.0)
 4/5
  • Quality Rating: 8.1/10
  • Expert Rating: 8.0/10
  • Users Rating: 7.9/10

Testers had high expectations for this serum from Clarins, a historic French beauty brand. Double Serum is one of the brand’s best-selling products, first produced in the early 80s and reformulated multiple times.

Double Serum contains 21 plant extracts, including turmeric, avocado and goji berry and some unique ingredients like the leaf of life plant. The company reports that this serum firms, moisturizes and increases radiance. However, it doesn’t contain the clinical ingredients we like to see in a serum, such as retinol or hyaluronic acid. And it contains artificial fragrance, which we prefer to avoid.

Pros

  • A top-selling serum from a heralded French beauty brand A top-selling serum from a heralded French beauty brand

  • Artificial fragrance is included in the formulation

  • No money-back guarantee on products purchased through the company’s website

#5 Obagi

Obagi Professional-C Serum
Overall Rating (3.8)
 3.8/5
  • Quality Rating: 7.6/10
  • Expert Rating: 7.5/10
  • Users Rating: 7.7/10

Obagi provides limited details about its vitamin C serums online. Professional C-Serum is indicated to reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles, but we weren’t able to find much more information about how the product works or guidance on how to use it.

Professional-C Serum options include formulas with 10, 15 and 20 percent l-ascorbic acid, a powerful form of vitamin C. But we found it difficult to navigate the company’s website and locate information about the product, such as features and benefits, ingredients, expected results and even the price. Without these basic details, testers would not recommend the product.

Pros

  • A reputable brand sold at doctors’ and dermatologists’ offices
  • Offers formulas with different percentages of vitamin C for various skin types and concerns
  • Loyalty program available with purchases made from a physician’s office

Cons

  • The company does not make the full ingredient list and comprehensive product information easily accessible on its website
  • Products cannot be purchased on the Obagi website and may require a special trip to a dermatologist
